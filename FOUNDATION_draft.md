# Articles of Association  
## The Fountain of Love — Non-Profit Association (vzw)

---

# Part 1 — Name, Seat, Purpose

---

### **Article 1 — Name and Legal Form**

1. A non-profit association is hereby established under the name **“The Fountain of Love”**, abbreviated as **FOL vzw**.  
2. The association is governed by the provisions of **Book 9 of the Belgian Code of Companies and Associations** and by these Articles of Association.  
3. All acts, invoices, announcements, publications, and other documents issued by the association shall state its name, preceded or followed by the words *“vereniging zonder winstoogmerk”* or the abbreviation *“vzw”*, as well as the address of its registered office.

---

### **Article 2 — Registered Office**

1. The registered office of the association is located in the **Flemish Region of Belgium**.  
   The specific address may be determined by decision of the Board of Directors and recorded in the minutes.  
2. The Board of Directors is empowered to relocate the registered office within the Flemish Region, provided such relocation does not require an amendment to the language of the Articles.  
   Any such change must be filed with the competent registry of the Commercial Court and published in the *Annexes to the Belgian Official Gazette*.  
3. The Board may also establish or dissolve **operational branches, regional offices, or project centres** both in Belgium and abroad, whenever necessary for the pursuit of the association’s objectives.

---

### **Article 3 — Purpose and Mission**

#### **1. General Purpose**

The association is a **non-profit organization** that operates for the **common good**.  
Its mission is to **strengthen the ethical, ecological, technological, and cultural foundations of a regenerative society**.  
It promotes cooperation between individuals, communities, institutions, and technologies in order to restore balance between people, planet, and systems.

#### **2. Objectives**

The association pursues the following objectives:

- **Promote social and environmental regeneration** through research, education, design, and community action.  
- **Encourage integrity, transparency, and accountability** in civic and organizational governance.  
- **Support technological innovation that serves the public interest**, emphasizing accessibility, security, and sustainability.  
- **Foster cultural literacy and collective learning** by sharing knowledge across disciplines, generations, and sectors.  
- **Enable cooperation across scales**—from local communities to international networks—by creating frameworks for ethical collaboration.  
- **Contribute to a resilient and just society** that aligns human development with ecological and technological systems.

#### **3. Methods and Activities**

To achieve these objectives, the association may undertake, without limitation:

- Conducting **research, design, and advisory projects** in the fields of social innovation, systems governance, and sustainable development.  
- Organizing **training, symposia, workshops, and public programs** that build civic capacity and ethical leadership.  
- Developing **digital tools and infrastructures** that enhance transparency, inclusion, and participatory decision-making.  
- Publishing **reports, media, and educational materials** that disseminate knowledge and evidence-based practices.  
- Forming **partnerships** with organizations, institutions, and networks that share similar purposes.  
- **Acquiring, managing, and utilizing resources**—financial, material, or intellectual—necessary for its work, in accordance with applicable laws.  
- Supporting or participating in **other organizations or consortia** whose objectives contribute to the association’s mission.

#### **4. Non-Profit Character**

1. The association does **not distribute profits** to its founders, members, directors, or any other persons.  
2. Any surplus is fully reinvested in the realization of the statutory purpose and in the maintenance or expansion of the association’s activities.  
3. In all its operations, the association upholds the principles of **ethical governance, transparency, and accountability**.

---

# Part 2 — Membership, Rights & Obligations, Termination

---

### **Article 4 — Membership**

1. **Categories of Members**  
   The association consists of **Effective Members** and **Associate Members**.  
   - *Effective Members* have full voting rights in the General Assembly.  
   - *Associate Members* may participate in activities and consultations but have no voting rights.

2. **Admission of Members**  
   - Any natural or legal person who supports the objectives of the association and commits to its ethical code may apply for membership.  
   - Applications are submitted in writing to the Board of Directors, which decides on admission by simple majority.  
   - The Board’s decision is final and does not require justification; it is communicated in writing to the applicant.

3. **Register of Members**  
   The association maintains an up-to-date **Register of Members**, containing the names, addresses, and contact details of all Effective Members.  
   This register is kept at the registered office and may be consulted by members in accordance with legal provisions.

4. **Representation of Legal Persons**  
   Any legal person admitted as a member must designate a **representative** authorized to exercise membership rights on its behalf.  
   Changes in representation must be communicated in writing to the Board.

5. **Non-Transferability**  
   Membership is **personal and non-transferable**. It cannot be assigned, pledged, or sold.

---

### **Article 5 — Rights and Obligations of Members**

1. **Rights of Members**  
   Each Effective Member has the right:  
   - To participate and vote in the General Assembly.  
   - To submit proposals or agenda items to the Board or Assembly in accordance with internal procedures.  
   - To be informed about the activities, financial status, and decisions of the association.  
   - To consult the association’s records, minutes, and financial statements upon request, in compliance with privacy and confidentiality rules.

2. **Obligations of Members**  
   All members commit to:  
   - Respecting the Articles of Association, internal regulations, and decisions of the governing bodies.  
   - Upholding the mission, values, and ethical standards of the association.  
   - Contributing actively, through participation or expertise, to the realization of the objectives.  
   - Paying any membership fees or contributions determined by the General Assembly.

3. **Ethical Conduct**  
   Members are expected to act with **integrity, respect, and responsibility** in all matters related to the association.  
   Actions that undermine the reputation or lawful interests of the association may lead to disciplinary measures or exclusion.

4. **Voluntary Engagement**  
   Membership does not create any employment relationship.  
   Activities undertaken by members are voluntary unless otherwise defined by contract or project agreement.

---

### **Article 6 — Termination of Membership**

1. **By Voluntary Resignation**  
   Any member may resign at any time by submitting written notice to the Board of Directors.  
   The resignation takes effect upon receipt of the notice unless another date is specified.  
   The resigning member remains liable for any obligations or contributions due before the resignation date.

2. **By Non-Compliance or Inactivity**  
   Membership may be terminated if a member:  
   - Fails to comply with statutory or internal obligations.  
   - Engages in conduct contrary to the association’s mission or ethical standards.  
   - Fails to participate in activities or pay required contributions for more than one year.  
   The Board decides on termination after hearing the member’s explanation.

3. **By Exclusion**  
   - The **General Assembly** may exclude an Effective Member for serious cause, upon proposal of the Board.  
   - The concerned member must be invited in writing to present a defense before the decision is taken.  
   - Exclusion requires a two-thirds majority of votes present or represented at the General Assembly.

4. **By Dissolution of a Legal Entity**  
   If a member that is a legal person ceases to exist, its membership automatically terminates.

5. **Effects of Termination**  
   - Resigning or excluded members have **no claim** to the assets of the association.  
   - They may not demand repayment of fees, donations, or contributions.  
   - Their obligations to the association up to the date of termination remain enforceable.

# Part 3 — Governance Structure, General Assembly, Board of Directors

---

### **Article 7 — Governance Structure**

1. **Governing Bodies**  
   The association is managed and represented through the following bodies:  
   - The **General Assembly**  
   - The **Board of Directors**  
   - Optional supporting or advisory bodies that may be created by decision of the Board (for example: committees, councils, or working groups).

2. **Delegation and Oversight**  
   - The General Assembly is the **highest decision-making authority** of the association.  
   - The Board of Directors is responsible for **operational management and legal representation**.  
   - Committees or coordinators act under delegated authority and report to the Board.

3. **Transparency and Accountability**  
   All governance bodies operate according to the principles of **transparency, participation, integrity, and collective responsibility**.  
   Their decisions are recorded in written minutes and stored at the registered office.

---

### **Article 8 — The General Assembly**

1. **Composition**  
   The General Assembly consists of all **Effective Members** of the association.  
   Each Effective Member has **one vote**.  
   Associate Members may attend in an advisory capacity but do not vote.

2. **Powers**  
   The General Assembly has exclusive authority to:  
   - Amend the Articles of Association.  
   - Appoint and dismiss members of the Board of Directors.  
   - Approve the annual budget, accounts, and activity report.  
   - Determine membership fees or contributions.  
   - Approve internal regulations and major strategic plans.  
   - Authorize the association’s participation in or withdrawal from other legal entities.  
   - Decide on the voluntary dissolution of the association and the allocation of its assets.  
   - Decide on the exclusion of Effective Members.

3. **Meetings**  
   - The **Ordinary General Assembly** is held at least **once per year**, within six months following the end of the financial year.  
   - **Extraordinary General Assemblies** may be convened by the Board of Directors whenever required by the interests of the association or at the written request of at least one fifth of the Effective Members.

4. **Notice and Agenda**  
   - Invitations are sent at least **fifteen days** before the meeting by email or letter, specifying the date, time, location, and agenda.  
   - Only items included on the agenda may be validly decided upon, except where all members are present and unanimously agree to deliberate additional items.

5. **Quorum and Voting**  
   - Decisions are valid when at least **half of the Effective Members** are present or represented.  
   - If the quorum is not reached, a second meeting may be convened with the same agenda, which will be valid regardless of attendance.  
   - Unless otherwise required by law or these Articles, resolutions are adopted by **simple majority** of votes cast.  
   - Amendments to the Articles or exclusion of members require a **two-thirds majority**; dissolution requires a **four-fifths majority**.

6. **Representation and Proxies**  
   - Members may be represented by another Effective Member holding a written proxy.  
   - Each member may hold a maximum of two proxies.

7. **Minutes**  
   - Minutes of the Assembly are drawn up by the Secretary, signed by the Chair and at least one Director, and kept at the registered office.  
   - Copies or extracts may be issued to third parties by the Chair or Secretary.

---

### **Article 9 — The Board of Directors**

1. **Composition and Appointment**  
   - The association is managed by a **Board of Directors** composed of at least **three members**, unless the total number of Effective Members is fewer than three, in which case the Board may consist of all of them.  
   - Directors are appointed by the General Assembly for a renewable term of **three years**.  
   - The Board elects among its members a **Chair**, **Secretary**, and **Treasurer**.

2. **Powers and Responsibilities**  
   The Board is vested with the **broadest powers** to manage and administer the association, except for matters reserved to the General Assembly by law or by these Articles.  
   Its responsibilities include, among others:  
   - Implementing the decisions of the General Assembly.  
   - Preparing the annual report, accounts, and budget.  
   - Managing staff, volunteers, and resources.  
   - Establishing policies, partnerships, and operational plans.  
   - Representing the association in legal and administrative matters.  
   - Ensuring compliance with applicable laws and ethical standards.

3. **Meetings**  
   - The Board meets as often as necessary, at least **four times per year**.  
   - Meetings are convened by the Chair or at the request of at least two Directors.  
   - Notice of meetings and the agenda are sent at least **seven days** in advance unless urgent circumstances require otherwise.

4. **Decision-Making**  
   - The Board can deliberate when at least **half of its members** are present or represented.  
   - Decisions are taken by **simple majority** of those present or represented.  
   - In case of a tie, the Chair has a casting vote.  
   - Urgent decisions may be taken by written or electronic consent, provided all Directors agree in writing.

5. **Representation of the Association**  
   - The association is legally represented by **two Directors acting jointly**, including the Chair or another Director designated by the Board.  
   - The Board may grant specific powers of attorney to one or more persons, Directors or not, for particular matters or categories of acts.

6. **Remuneration**  
   Directors perform their duties **without remuneration**, except for the reimbursement of duly justified expenses.

7. **Resignation and Termination**  
   - A Director may resign at any time by written notice to the Chair.  
   - The General Assembly may remove a Director at any time, with or without cause.  
   - If a vacancy occurs, the Board may temporarily appoint a replacement until the next General Assembly.

8. **Liability**  
   Directors carry out their duties with diligence and good faith and are responsible for the consequences of serious or repeated misconduct, fraud, or legal violations in accordance with Belgian law.

# Part 4 — Financial Management, Representation, Internal Regulations, Dissolution

---

### **Article 10 — Financial Management and Accounts**

1. **Financial Year**  
   - The financial year of the association begins on **January 1** and ends on **December 31** of each year.  
   - The first financial year begins on the date of establishment and ends on the following December 31.

2. **Annual Accounts and Budget**  
   - At the close of each financial year, the **Board of Directors** prepares the **annual accounts** and the **budget for the next year**.  
   - Both documents are submitted to the **General Assembly** for approval within six months after the end of the financial year.

3. **Accounting Standards**  
   - The association maintains its accounts in accordance with the **legal provisions applicable to non-profit associations in Belgium**.  
   - Depending on its size, it may use simplified, double-entry, or full accounting systems as prescribed by law.

4. **Auditing and Supervision**  
   - When legally required, the General Assembly appoints an **auditor** or an **external accountant** to review the financial statements.  
   - The auditor’s report is presented together with the annual accounts for approval.

5. **Financial Oversight and Transparency**  
   - All funds, donations, and assets must be used solely for the realization of the statutory purpose.  
   - The Board ensures the **integrity, traceability, and responsible use of resources**, and provides transparent reporting to members and partners.

6. **Liability and Risk Management**  
   - The association maintains appropriate **financial and legal safeguards**, including insurance and risk management procedures.  
   - Directors are not personally liable for the association’s obligations, except in cases of gross negligence or legal violation.

---

### **Article 11 — Legal Representation and Authorizations**

1. **General Representation**  
   - The association is legally represented by **two Directors acting jointly**, including the Chair or another Director designated by the Board.  
   - These representatives may undertake all legal acts necessary to achieve the association’s purpose, including managing funds, signing contracts, and representing the association before public authorities or in court.

2. **Special Authorizations**  
   - The Board may delegate specific powers or tasks to one or more individuals, Directors or not, for defined purposes or time periods.  
   - These powers of attorney must be in writing and specify the scope of authority.

3. **Daily Management**  
   - The Board may appoint a **Managing Director** or **Coordinator** responsible for daily operations.  
   - The scope of daily management includes routine administrative actions, financial transactions of limited value, and operational decisions.  
   - The Managing Director may represent the association for acts within this scope.

4. **Conflict of Interest**  
   - Directors must declare any **potential or actual conflicts of interest** and abstain from deliberation or voting on related matters.  
   - All such declarations and measures are recorded in the minutes.

---

### **Article 12 — Internal Regulations**

1. **Purpose and Scope**  
   - The association may adopt **Internal Regulations** to specify procedures and guidelines not covered by these Articles of Association.  
   - These may include: membership procedures, governance structures, ethical codes, financial controls, and project management policies.

2. **Adoption and Amendment**  
   - Internal Regulations are drafted by the Board of Directors and approved by the General Assembly.  
   - Amendments require a simple majority of votes cast at a duly convened meeting.

3. **Compliance and Enforcement**  
   - Internal Regulations must always comply with the law and these Articles.  
   - In case of contradiction, the Articles of Association prevail.  
   - All members are required to adhere to the Internal Regulations once adopted.

---

### **Article 13 — Dissolution and Liquidation**

1. **Voluntary Dissolution**  
   - The association may be voluntarily dissolved by decision of the **General Assembly**, taken in accordance with legal requirements and by a **four-fifths majority** of votes.  
   - The Assembly appoints one or more **liquidators** and determines their powers and remuneration, if any.

2. **Assets and Liabilities**  
   - Upon dissolution, all debts and obligations of the association are settled before any distribution of remaining assets.  
   - Any net assets that remain after liquidation must be **transferred to one or more non-profit organizations** pursuing similar purposes of public benefit.  
   - Under no circumstances may the assets be distributed to members, founders, or Directors.

3. **Legal Dissolution**  
   - In cases of legal dissolution, insolvency, or inactivity, the association follows the procedures set out in the **Belgian Code of Companies and Associations**.

4. **Archival Preservation**  
   - All official records, minutes, and financial statements are archived in accordance with legal requirements for at least **seven years** following liquidation.

5. **Continuity of Purpose**  
   - Whenever possible, the association ensures that its ongoing projects, data, and intellectual contributions are **transferred to a successor entity** that upholds the same ethical and regenerative principles.
